Conway's Game of Life

Welcome to Conway's Game of Life.
Conway's game of life was developed by British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. The rules are simple. Below is an organism composed of individual cells. A white cell is 'dead' and a black cell is 'alive'. If any dead cell has three neighboring cells that are alive, it will be brought to life upon the next iteration. If a cell is already alive, two or three of its neighbors must also be alive for it to remain alive on the next iteration. Please press the button to begin life.
